<threat_model>
Boundary: untrusted provider JSONL/parser failures become daemon terminal data and then enter extension diagnostics/log storage.

- Authority: the production parser owns typed `AgentProtocolDriftError`; supervisor maps only its closed `reason` to sanitized terminal labels; background accepts only the authoritative final; the reporter alone controls ring admission.
- Exact keys and bounds: terminal/reporter detail has exactly `adapterId`, `expected`, and `observed`; Phase 62 accepts only `claude-code`, the five expected labels above, a closed observed reason set, and strings at most 64 characters. Reject unknown/missing keys and non-data properties.
- Malformed/prototype failure: reject inherited keys, accessors, poisoned prototypes, arrays, functions, generic error objects, malformed finals, and unshipped adapter IDs. A generic `agent_protocol_drift` retains fixed safe labels rather than copying `message`, stack, issue paths, or arbitrary fields.
- Forbidden data: never log or transport raw JSONL, parser line/index, issue paths, provider message/output, prompt/task text, binary path/version, environment data, session/delegation IDs, bridge secrets, tokens, error message/stack/cause, or caller-supplied context.
- Idempotence/concurrency: supervisor settles/cleans each child once and emits no success result after drift. Background remembers at most 512 authoritative delegation IDs with FIFO eviction and calls the reporter at most once per delegation; IDs are never logged. Reporter tracks a bounded per-adapter timestamp map, emits at t=0, suppresses elapsed times below 10,000 ms, admits at exactly 10,000 ms, and treats clock rollback as a reset-and-admit event.
- Negative tests: every typed reason family, generic drift, hostile detail, secret/redaction canary, duplicate/replayed final, reconnect/panel reopen, different delegation, t=0/boundary/rollback clocks, missing/throwing sink, and child cleanup/settlement races.
- Security gate: unresolved High/Critical raw-data leak, malformed-detail acceptance, ring-entry-on-suppression, duplicate report, or double settlement blocks completion.
</threat_model>
